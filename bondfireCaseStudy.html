Bondfire

Digital platform that allows Youtube creators to earn additional revenue by taking on challenges, new ideas and Q&As made by their fans.

Problem

For Youtubers creators to be able to financially sustain themselves as a full-time role is their biggest concern. The following data presents the learnings from Bondfire v1 which was a chat platform for creators to engage with their fans.

Lack of sustainable income

  • Demonetisation
  • Adsense $$$ taken by Youtube
  • Younger audience can’t donate as much as they want to

No direct time and effort correlation

  • Engaging with fans is amazing but does not pay for bills

Solution

Creating a playground for creators to interact with their fans with monetary rewards for their time was the hypothesis we came up and it would test:

Creating Quests

  • Testing the usability of fans creating ‘quests’/challenges with their favourite Youtube creator.

Business Model

  • The coin system. To test the business model as to whether or not fans are will to watch some ads to earn ‘coins’.

3. Design

Components
Our choice of icon and component design would follow Material UI design by Google as there is already enough documentation and industry based standards around how to produce clean UI.

This was a section that I was able to add front-end development value with the understanding of CSS and React components. By adding notes on the components, allowed both myself and the engineer to follow simple instructions on how to format the UI Components for future reference and development.
